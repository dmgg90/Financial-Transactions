Customer Spending & Financial Behavior Analysis
Overview

This project analyzes customer spending behavior using transaction and demographic data to identify relationships between income, debt, age, gender, and spending patterns.

Using Python, SQLite, and Power BI, the data was cleaned, transformed, and modeled to create an interactive dashboard that explores how different customer groups manage debt and spending.

The project focuses on understanding customer financial behavior and identifying spending trends across demographic segments.

Business Problem

Financial institutions need to understand how customer demographics influence spending habits and debt accumulation in order to:

Improve customer segmentation strategies
Better understand spending behavior
Identify high-value customer groups
Evaluate the relationship between income and debt
Support data-driven financial decision making

Key Findings
Debt Concentration

Customers between 30 and 49 years old carry the highest average debt levels across both genders while not having the highest income levels.

Income and Debt Relationship

Higher-income customers tend to accumulate more debt; however, debt does not increase proportionally with spending levels.

Spending Patterns

Customers with higher salaries generally spend significantly more than lower-income groups.

Potential Outliers

A small number of customers display unusually high spending despite relatively low income and debt levels, indicating potential anomalies worthy of further investigation.
